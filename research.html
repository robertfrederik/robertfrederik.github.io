A representation-theoretic approach to quantum error correction for qudits
Prof Dorian Gangloff, University of Cambridge, Summer 2024
Preprint: arXiv:2410.02407 (To appear in Physical Review A)
Building upon the work done by Gross for \(\mathrm{SU}(2)\) and Herbert et al. for \(\mathrm{SU}(3)\), we formulated a general procedure for constructing quantum error-correcting codes through the irreducible representations of \(\mathrm{SU}(d)\) for any odd integer \(d \geq 3\). To do this, we used techniques from character theory to obtain the branching rules for \(\mathrm{SU}(d) \downarrow \mathrm{HW}(d)\), which enabled us to identify which symmetric vector spaces \(\mathrm{Sym}^N(\mathbb{C}^d)\) contain valid code spaces. By exploiting two forms of permutation invariance and the Heisenberg-Weyl symmetry of \(\mathfrak{su}(d)\), we substantially simplified the Knill-Laflamme conditions, thereby allowing us to devise a general recipe for constructing error-correcting codes for qudits. Finally, we demonstrated the use of our procedure by explicitly constructing an infinite class of codes that encode a logical qudit into \((d-1)^2\) physical qudits.
